Research Interests

My research focuses on inferring structured representations of the physical world—such as geometry, motion, illumination, and material appearance from images and video. I am particularly interested in radiance fields, 4D dynamic scene reconstruction, and hybrid explicit-implicit representations. A central objective of my work is to develop compact, temporally consistent, and interpretable models that can reconstruct complex scenes under challenging conditions such as occlusion and sparse supervision. I also explore the use of diffusion models for fine-grained refinement and uncertainty-aware reasoning in dynamic environments.

SHaDe: Compact and Consistent Dynamic 3D Reconstruction via Tri-Plane Deformation and Latent Diffusion
Asrar Alruwayqi
CVPR 2025 Workshops: 4D Vision & Neural Fields Beyond Cameras
project page / arXiv

SHaDe is a modular framework for MLP-free dynamic 3D reconstruction that introduces structured tri-plane deformation, SH-based radiance attention, and temporally consistent refinement via latent diffusion. It achieves compact and high-fidelity 4D reconstruction even under motion and occlusion.
