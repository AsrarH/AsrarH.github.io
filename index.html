I am a Researcher working at the intersection of computer vision, 3D geometry, and AI. I earned my Master’s in Computer Vision from Carnegie Mellon University's Robotics Institute, where I worked with Shubham Tulsiani, Fernando De la Torre, László A. Jeni, and Jessica Hodgins.

Research Interests

My research focuses on inferring structured representations of the physical world including geometry, motion, lighting, and material properties from images and video.. I am particularly interested in radiance fields, 4D dynamic scene reconstruction, and hybrid explicit-implicit representations. A central goal of my work is to enable temporally consistent, compact, and interpretable reconstructions of shape, motion, lighting, and material appearance especially under occlusion or limited supervision. I also explore the integration of diffusion models for refinement and uncertainty-aware prediction.

Projects

SHaDe: Compact and Consistent Dynamic 3D Reconstruction via Tri-Plane Deformation and Latent Diffusion
Asrar Alruwayqi
CVPR 2025 Workshops: 4D Vision & Neural Fields Beyond Cameras
project page / arXiv

SHaDe is a modular framework for MLP-free dynamic 3D reconstruction that introduces structured tri-plane deformation, SH-based radiance attention, and temporally consistent refinement via latent diffusion. It achieves compact and high-fidelity 4D reconstruction even under motion and occlusion.
